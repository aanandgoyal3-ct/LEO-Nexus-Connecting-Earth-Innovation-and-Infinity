What is Low Earth Orbit (LEO)?

LEO means space that is close to Earth — roughly between 160 km and 2,000 km above us. Because it is near, satellites in LEO are good for fast communications, detailed Earth pictures, science experiments in microgravity, and tests for future deeper space missions.

Why LEO matters

  • Low delay for signals (good for internet and calls).
  • Cheaper and easier to reach than farther orbits.
  • Great place to test new technology before going to the Moon or Mars.

NASA’s approach

NASA is moving from owning big stations like the ISS to buying services from private companies that build and run new stations. This makes space research cheaper and lets many companies and countries join. (See NASA LEO Microgravity Strategy). :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

How students can think about LEO

Imagine LEO like a busy sea of small islands (satellites and stations). Each island can host labs, relays for internet, or factories that make special materials in microgravity. Your student project — a modular hub — can plug into this sea and offer services to many users.
