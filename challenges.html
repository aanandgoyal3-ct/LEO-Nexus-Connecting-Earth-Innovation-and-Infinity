Main Challenges and Practical Problems

Space debris (big problem)

Many broken objects orbit Earth. If we do not remove or manage them, collisions make more debris. NASA studies and recommends ways to reduce and remove debris. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Student idea: show a small animation of "collision chain" to explain Kessler-like growth.

Cost & business model

Building and launching modules is expensive. NASA wants private companies to run stations so many customers can share costs. This is the commercial LEO idea. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Regulation & coordination

Many countries and companies must agree on radio rules, collision avoidance, and who pays for cleanup. This is hard but needed for safe space use.

Power, thermal, and lifetime

Radiation, hot-cold cycles, and micrometeoroids damage parts. Regular servicing can solve some of these but needs standard ports and trained robots.

How to reduce problems — simple steps

  • Design satellites to deorbit within 25 years, or add a small drag sail. (NASA guidance). :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
  • Use modular designs and in-orbit servicing to repair rather than discard hardware.
  • Share data between operators for collision avoidance (Space Traffic Management).
  • Encourage many small demonstrations first — cheaper testing lowers risk.
