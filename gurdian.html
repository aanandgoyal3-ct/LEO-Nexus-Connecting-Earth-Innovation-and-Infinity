Orbital Guardian — a student modular hub

Simple idea: a modular hub in LEO that focuses on safety and service. Each module has one job and can be launched separately.

Power Module

Large solar panels + batteries. Supplies other modules with electricity through a standard power connector.

Lab Module

Small lab space for student experiments, microgravity tests, and sensors.

Service / Refuel Module

A docking port for small robots and refueling craft to extend life and perform repairs.

Deorbit Module

Carries a drag-sail or a small thruster package that attaches to retired modules to safely lower them to burn up in the atmosphere. This follows NASA’s deorbit ideas. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

AI & Control

Onboard computer watches sensors and tells robots what to do if something breaks. Helps avoid collisions and manage power automatically.

Standard Docking Port

A simple, agreed connector (mechanical + electrical) that lets different craft attach to the hub safely.

How Orbital Guardian helps people on Earth

  • Better climate data from sensors in LEO → improved weather and disaster warnings.
  • Faster internet & communications when used as relay nodes.
  • New science and making special materials that can help medicine and technology.
