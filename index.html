About Low Earth Orbit

Low Earth Orbit (LEO) spans altitudes from 160 km to 2,000 km. Its proximity enables low-latency communication, reduced launch costs, and frequent missions. It’s home to most satellites serving connectivity, monitoring, and research.

Why LEO Matters

Global Communication: High-speed internet for all.
Advanced Research: Microgravity experiments.
Climate Monitoring: Data for climate change.
National Security: Surveillance & secure comms.
